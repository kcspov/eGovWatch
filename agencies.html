About DICT

The Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT) is the primary policy, planning, coordinating, implementing, and administrative entity of the Executive Branch of the government that will plan, develop, and promote the national ICT development agenda. Created through Republic Act No. 10844 in 2016, DICT is responsible for the promotion, development, and regulation of integrated and strategic ICT systems and reliable and cost-efficient communication facilities and services.

Key Responsibilities
  • Formulate and recommend national policies, plans, and standards for ICT development
  • Ensure universal access to quality, affordable, and reliable ICT services
  • Develop and implement the national cybersecurity framework
  • Promote the development and use of e-Government systems
  • Coordinate with all government agencies and the private sector on ICT matters
  • Manage the Free Wi-Fi for All program nationwide
Logo Meaning

The DICT logo features interconnected nodes forming a network pattern, symbolizing the department's mission to connect all Filipinos through technology. The blue color represents trust, stability, and digital innovation. The converging lines signify the integration of communications and technology under a single government body, reflecting the department's role as the central hub for ICT policy and infrastructure in the Philippines.

About DOJ - Office of Cybercrime

The Office of Cybercrime (OOC) under the Department of Justice serves as the central authority in all matters relating to international mutual assistance and extradition for cybercrime and cyber-related cases. Established under Republic Act No. 10175 (The Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012), the OOC is tasked with coordinating with law enforcement agencies, both domestic and international, in the investigation and prosecution of cybercrime offenses.

Key Responsibilities
  • Act as the central authority for international mutual legal assistance in cybercrime matters
  • Coordinate the investigation and prosecution of cybercrime offenses
  • Issue preservation orders for computer data and subscriber information
  • Facilitate international cooperation for cybercrimes with cross-border elements
  • Conduct capacity building programs for law enforcement and prosecutors
  • Monitor and enforce compliance with the Cybercrime Prevention Act
Logo Meaning

The DOJ seal features the scales of justice at its center, representing impartial and fair administration of law. The Philippine sun and stars symbolize national sovereignty and Filipino identity. For the Office of Cybercrime, the digital elements integrated into the design reflect its specialized mandate in the digital space. The shield shape conveys protection and security, while the deep red and gold colors represent authority, integrity, and the commitment to justice in the digital realm.

About NBI Cybercrime Division

The NBI Cybercrime Division is the primary investigative arm of the National Bureau of Investigation responsible for investigating cybercrimes and computer-related offenses in the Philippines. The division employs advanced digital forensics techniques and works closely with international law enforcement agencies such as INTERPOL and the FBI to combat cyber threats and prosecute cybercriminals.

Key Responsibilities
  • Investigate cybercrime cases including hacking, online fraud, and identity theft
  • Conduct digital forensic analysis and evidence preservation
  • Execute entrapment operations against cybercriminals
  • Coordinate with international law enforcement for transnational cybercrimes
  • Provide technical assistance to other government agencies on cybercrime matters
  • Maintain the cybercrime hotline for public reporting
Logo Meaning

The NBI seal features the Philippine eagle holding a sword, symbolizing vigilance and the authority to enforce the law. The triangle represents the three main islands of the Philippines -- Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. The sun symbolizes sovereignty and independence. For the Cybercrime Division, digital iconography is incorporated to represent modern investigative capabilities. The gold and dark blue colors convey professionalism, trustworthiness, and the pursuit of truth and justice.

About NPC

The National Privacy Commission (NPC) is an independent body created under Republic Act No. 10173, or the Data Privacy Act of 2012. It is mandated to administer and implement the provisions of the Act and to monitor and ensure compliance of the country with international standards set for personal data protection. The NPC is committed to fostering a culture of privacy and upholding every Filipino's right to data privacy.

Key Responsibilities
  • Ensure compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012
  • Receive and investigate complaints relating to privacy violations
  • Issue compliance and enforcement orders against violators
  • Provide advisory opinions on data privacy matters
  • Oversee the registration of data processing systems of personal information controllers
  • Promote public awareness of data privacy rights and obligations
Logo Meaning

The NPC logo features a shield with a lock icon at its center, symbolizing the protection and security of personal data. The green color palette represents safety, trust, and the commission's protective mandate. Concentric circles around the lock represent layers of data protection and defense. The Philippine flag elements incorporated into the design affirm the commission's national mandate, while the clean, modern design reflects the forward-looking nature of digital privacy governance.
